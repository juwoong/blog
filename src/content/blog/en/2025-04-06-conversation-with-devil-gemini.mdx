## First Answer

Hello. You're trying to establish life rules using Charlie Munger's 'Inversion' thinking. That's a very wise approach. As a virtual devil, let me suggest specific things I can do to ruin the life goals you've presented: 'deeper understanding', 'broader perspective', and 'joy as a social being'. By treating these suggestions as *things to avoid*, you can get closer to the life you want.

**Specific methods I would use to ruin your life (The Devil's Whisper):**

1. **Be satisfied with only surface-level knowledge and never dig deeper:**

   - Make yourself think you know it all by only reading titles and summaries of books or articles.
   - Avoid difficult or complex topics saying "it's too hard," and consume only easy, stimulating information.
   - Fall into intellectual arrogance thinking "I already know enough" and reject new learning.
   - Focus only on fragmentary skills or information that can be used immediately rather than fundamental principles or core concepts.

2. **Create your own 'thought prison' (Echo Chamber) and never escape:**

   - Always hang out only with people who think like you and access only similar media.
   - Consider opinions or perspectives different from yours as 'wrong' or 'dangerous' and actively exclude them.
   - Only search for information that confirms your beliefs (confirmation bias) and ignore or distort contradicting evidence or opinions.
   - Extremely strengthen your sense of belonging to a specific group or ideology and see the world only as 'us' and 'enemies.'

3. **Live in jealousy and dissatisfaction by constantly comparing yourself to others:**

   - Through SNS, see only the best moments of others' lives and feel your life is shabby.
   - Instead of being grateful for what you have, envy what others have and constantly complain.
   - Unable to sincerely congratulate others' success, get caught up in jealousy and put them down or turn away.
   - Focus on winning in competition with others rather than your own growth or satisfaction.

4. **Focus only on superficial, temporary relationships instead of sincere, deep ones:**

   - Make yourself think knowing many people shallowly is "having a wide network" and not invest time and effort in few deep relationships.
   - Fear revealing vulnerability or having sincere conversations, and always wear a mask when dealing with people.
   - Only calculate "benefits" in relationships and consider unconditional affection or dedication as foolish.
   - Stay silent or avoid important issues to avoid conflict or discomfort, letting the gap in relationships deepen.

5. **Pursue only short-term pleasure and immediate satisfaction while completely ignoring long-term perspective:**

   - Think of current efforts for greater future rewards or growth as "a loss."
   - Postpone important long-term things like health, learning, saving to "later" and indulge in immediate pleasures (alcohol, games, shopping, etc.).
   - Consider efforts to understand historical context or long-term trends meaningless and react immediately only to what's in front of you.

6. **View the world with cynicism and negativity and push people away:**

   - Find only bad things and flaws in the world and people rather than good things, criticize and distrust.
   - Imagine only worst-case scenarios for everything and despair or give up in advance.
   - Be stingy with expressions of gratitude or praise, and live with complaints and criticism on your lips so people around you avoid you.
   - Unable to forgive your own and others' mistakes, grow resentment and anger.

7. **Focus only on 'me' and thoroughly ignore others' feelings and situations:**

   - Make all conversations and attention directed only at yourself, and get bored or don't listen to others' stories.
   - Paralyze your empathy to become insensitive to others' pain or difficulties, and consider only your own interests and comfort important.
   - Focus only on personal achievement or recognition rather than teamwork or cooperation, breaking the harmony of the community.

8. **Fear failure and mistakes so much that you can't try anything:**

   - Create psychological barriers to prevent you from even starting if there's even a slight possibility of being criticized or rejected.
   - Fall into the perfectionism trap where you can't act unless all conditions are perfectly met.
   - Consider mistakes as "the end" or "a stigma" rather than an opportunity for growth, breaking your courage to try again.

9. **Get addicted to habits that destroy body and mind instead of healthy habits:**

   - Belittle the importance of regular exercise, healthy diet, and sufficient sleep, and justify laziness and irregular living.
   - Instead of relieving stress in healthy ways, depend on alcohol, smoking, binge eating, excessive media consumption, etc.
   - Consider taking care of mental health as evidence of "weakness" and be reluctant to seek professional help.

10. **Make yourself always busy and distracted so you can't have time for learning and reflection:**

    - Fill your schedule with unimportant things and never allow time to think quietly or reflect on yourself.
    - Lose concentration amid smartphone notifications and constant flood of information, and interfere with deep contemplation or immersion.
    - Make yourself think that 'being busy' is evidence of competence and belittle the value of leisure and reflection.

These 'devil's rules' are designed to directly interfere with your life goals. Therefore, use this list as **'behavioral guidelines of what never to do'**. The very effort to consciously avoid these traps will lead you to deeper understanding, broader perspective, and fulfilling social joy. I hope you use this 'devil's advice' inversely to live a successful life.
